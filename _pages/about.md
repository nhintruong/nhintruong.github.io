Hi, I'm Nhi. I love building and evaluating machine learning systems. I currently work with Professor [Sanmi Koyejo](https://cs.stanford.edu/~sanmi/) at the [Stanford Trustworthy AI Research Lab](https://ai.stanford.edu/), where I help develop evaluation frameworks for large language models, and this summer I'm joining [Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/) as a research intern. My work has been published at NAACL and ICLR, and featured in [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/26/technology/ai-language-gap.html) and the [HAI news room](https://hai.stanford.edu/news/improving-equity-and-access-non-english-large-language-models).

I study Computer Science and Economics at [DePauw University](https://www.depauw.edu/), where I'm an [Honor Scholar](https://www.depauw.edu/academics/honors-fellows-programs/honor-scholar-program/), a [Management Fellow](https://www.depauw.edu/academics/honors-fellows-programs/management-fellows/), a [Science Research Fellow](https://www.depauw.edu/academics/honors-fellows-programs/science-research-fellows/), and part of the [Information Technology Associates Program](https://www.depauw.edu/it/itap/).

Previously, I worked on time series forecasting at the [VNU-HCMUT AI Lab](https://www.hcmut.edu.vn/en) and contributed to [The R Project](https://www.r-project.org/) through Google Summer of Code.

My interests are in machine learning, natural language processing, and time series forecasting, especially their applications in healthcare and education. Feel free to [reach out](mailto:nntruong@cs.stanford.edu).
